# `paper4-higgs-vev-debt` — Higgs VEV magnitude explanatory debt

**Status:** OPEN
**Paper:** Paper 4 § 6.3 (v5.1)
**Domain:** Physics (particle theory; quantum foundations)
**Time estimate:** unbounded; open research engagement

---

## Background

Paper 4 proposes that physical symmetry breaking is a decoherence-scale description of an already-asymmetric process, with the *existence* of the symmetric phase reframed as a modeling artifact. § 6.3 applies this framing to the electroweak Higgs mechanism: the Higgs vacuum expectation value (VEV, ≈ 246 GeV) is the scale at which electroweak symmetry is broken.

The paper explicitly flags that while the process-primacy framing gives an account of *why* there is a broken phase at all — asymmetric process is ontologically prior, decoherence-visible symmetry is derivative — it does not re-derive the specific magnitude 246 GeV. The magnitude remains an empirical input whose origin is not addressed by the framing.

## The claim

Paper 4 § 6.3 (v5.1) acknowledges this as an **explanatory debt**: the re-framing answers a qualitative question ("why is there a broken phase?") but not a quantitative one ("why this scale?").

## What's being asked

The paper does not ask a validator to resolve this debt. It asks:

1. **Is the debt correctly characterized?** Is it fair to say the process-primacy framing owes the Higgs VEV magnitude (and, by extension, the pattern of Standard-Model Yukawa couplings and CP violation asymmetry) and currently provides no derivation of it?
2. **Is the debt load-bearing for the paper's claims?** If yes, what would have to change for the framing to be viable? If no, what lesser claim is the paper defensibly making?
3. **Is there precedent from quantum foundations or cosmology that makes this kind of debt acceptable?** For example, do Penrose's Weyl curvature hypothesis or Prigogine's process-metaphysics carry analogous debts, and how are they handled there?

## What a resolution would look like

A resolution could take any of several forms:

- **Acceptance of the debt as-is.** A physicist confirms that the paper's characterization is fair, the debt is load-bearing but not fatal, and the framing stands as a philosophical / foundational proposal pending quantitative extension.
- **Correction of the characterization.** The debt is misdescribed (e.g., the paper conflates VEV magnitude with VEV existence in a way that a working physicist would correct), and the § 6.3 text should be rewritten.
- **Partial re-derivation.** Some fraction of the Higgs VEV — perhaps its order of magnitude, or its relationship to the Planck scale — can be recovered from the process-primacy framing, and the paper should state that recovery specifically.
- **Rejection.** The re-framing does not actually explain the existence of broken phases either, and the paper's § 6.3 claim is overstated.

## Why this claim is distinctive

Most claims in the paper series are mathematical or philosophical. This one is physical. It is the single site where the framework makes a specific claim about Standard-Model physics, and the paper is honest that it does not discharge the quantitative obligation.

Validation here is not a one-hour check; it is an invitation to a working quantum foundationalist or particle theorist to comment on whether the framing is worth developing further.

## Background

The programme's central structural claim is that any domain of practice organized around a self-limiting generative operation — a **kernel** satisfying the four criteria of prior, monogenic, inescapable, and self-limiting — admits exactly **five structurally distinct practitioner positions**: Infrastructure, Distribution, Exploitation, Commitment, Refusal. Paper 1 § 3.4 gives a verbal derivation through a structured decision tree (one binary distinction on whether the comma is encountered; a three-way partition of the encounter branch into field, geometry, and limit; a further binary on direction-of-approach to the limit). Paper 3 § 4 gives a category-theoretic sketch through D1–D4 (non-triviality, non-idempotence, fixed-point structure, comma generation) and points toward but does not carry out a formalization of the derivation itself.

Neither treatment states the schema tightly enough for a category theorist to take a stance on. The current state is: a verbal argument that the five positions are exhaustive (Paper 1), plus a categorical sketch that describes the comma but does not define the five positions over it (Paper 3). The programme's cross-domain claim — that the same five positions appear across music, cinema, architecture, literature, software, and physics because the derivation applies wherever a kernel exists — is load-bearing, and its strength depends on whether the derivation actually holds as a categorical fact rather than as a suggestive pattern.

This claim file closes the Level-1 gap: it states the five-position schema as a precise categorical object, with each position defined by a structural predicate over a single carrier (C, F, L, α); it works through three concrete instantiations (pure math, music, physics) in enough detail that a specialist can check whether each is coherent; and it names the tight spots where the schema is currently loose. The comma is denoted L throughout, matching Paper 1 § 3.4's existing convention and avoiding collision with the music kernel's functor (also named D in existing claim files) and with Paper 3 § 4's D-numbered conditions. It does **not** claim Level 2 (that exactly these five positions are forced by the categorical structure — that is the derivation *theorem*, which would require a category theorist's engagement and is named here as a downstream open problem, not claimed as established). The object of this file is the schema, not the theorem.

The distinction between Level 1 (precise definitional object) and Level 2 (derivation theorem) is the single most important scoping commitment of this file. A validator who concludes "the schema as stated is coherent but the derivation theorem is not established by this file" is giving the expected response.

## The claim

### Setup

Let `(C, F, L, α)` denote:

- **C**: a category (the ambient category in which the domain lives);
- **F : C → C**: an endofunctor (the kernel, viewed as the minimal generative operation of the domain);
- **L ⊂ C**: a distinguished subcategory or sub-class of objects (the **comma** — the locus of F's self-limit: the structural obstruction generated by F itself when its rules are followed to their limit). The notation `L` matches Paper 1 § 3.4's existing convention for the comma and avoids collision with Paper 3 § 4's D-numbered conditions and with the music kernel's functor `D` (`music-kernel-02-fixed-points.md`);
- **α : X → F(X)**: an F-coalgebra (a practitioner's work, construed as a carrier X together with F's action α).

The programme's D1–D3 conditions (Paper 3 § 4) constrain F to satisfy non-triviality, non-idempotence, and fixed-point structure, producing the comma L as the locus where F's self-limit surfaces. The four kernel criteria (Paper 1 § 3; Paper 3 § 8) constrain the admissible F's further to those which are prior, monogenic, inescapable, and self-limiting in the domain's own terms. The setup assumes these constraints are satisfied; the schema below operates on (C, F, L) satisfying them.

### Schema

For a coalgebra α : X → F(X), define five structural predicates. Throughout, "image(α)" abbreviates the subobject of F(X) generated by α's action, viewed as an object of C; "iterated reach of α" abbreviates the colimit of the chain X → F(X) → F²(X) → ... when it exists, otherwise the diagram itself.

**1. Infrastructure (Inf).**

*Predicate:* image(α) ⊂ C \ L — α's direct structural action does not engage L. Additionally, where the practitioner iterates F across the work's actual extent (n applications of F for some bounded n determined by the work), the iterated reach {X, F(X), F²(X), ..., Fⁿ(X)} stays in C \ L. No closure under unbounded F-iteration is required; Inf is characterized by the work's actual reach remaining below the comma, not by the existence of an ideal F-closed subobject.

*Intuition:* The practitioner works strictly within F's field below the comma. The kernel's own rules operate, but the self-limit is never reached across the extent of the work; L is structurally present in the enclosing C but operationally invisible to α's action.

**2. Distribution (Dist).**

*Predicate:* image(α) ∩ L is non-empty (the work engages L), image(α) is not entirely contained in L (the work also has structure outside L — distinguishing Dist from Expl), and α's intersection with L is not concentrated at a single connected component of L — α spans at least two distinct components of L.

*Intuition:* The practitioner encounters the comma and responds by distributing F's action across multiple locations of L, converting a single point of structural failure into a spread of managed tension. The comma is not resolved; it is redistributed.

**3. Exploitation (Expl).**

*Predicate:* α factors through the inclusion L ↪ C — the coalgebra's structural action lies entirely within the comma, and the practitioner uses L's internal geometry as generative material.

*Intuition:* The comma is the engine, not the obstruction. The practitioner's structural move depends constructively on L — without the comma, the work does not exist.

**4. Commitment (Comm).**

*Predicate:* α is an F-coalgebra that participates in a filtered diagram of F-coalgebras whose colimit (when it exists in C) names the limit point L obstructs, and whose canonical maps form a cone over that limit. When F has a terminal coalgebra ν in Coalg(F), α is a coalgebra morphism into a cone whose apex would be ν; when F has no terminal coalgebra (the generic case for the programme's kernels — see `music-kernel-03-terminal-coalgebra.md`), α is a coalgebra whose extended diagram attempts to construct such an apex as a colimit that is not attained in C, and Commitment is characterized by the universal property of the diagram rather than by the existence of an apex.

*Intuition:* The practitioner follows F's own rules toward the limit point L names, without abandoning F. Commitment is the structural position where the kernel is pushed to its own limit, producing a work whose coherence depends on extending F rather than exploiting or refusing it.

**5. Refusal (Ref).**

*Predicate:* The practitioner does not articulate the work as an F-coalgebra. There exists an endofunctor G : C → C such that (i) the work is structured as a G-coalgebra (carrier in C may coincide with an F-coalgebra carrier, but the operative structure map is G's, not F's); (ii) G is obtained from F by a **specific structural modification** — typically the dropping or negation of one of D1–D3 (most commonly the self-limiting / fixed-point structure that produces L); (iii) G's existence as an alternative generator is the practitioner's announced structural argument. The work is meaningful only against the background of F as the domain's default kernel.

*Intuition:* The practitioner refuses F as a generative principle and operates with a modified generator G. Refusal is the structural position that is only legible against F — it is meaningful because F is the default condition of the domain. At the carrier level, a Refusal-work may share C with F-coalgebras; at the generative-structure level, it disclaims F.

### Exhaustiveness: deferred

Whether the five predicates above are pairwise distinct and jointly exhaustive over the space of (C, F)-structured works satisfying D1–D3 is the content of the **five-position derivation theorem** (Level 2). Paper 1 § 3.4's decision-tree argument is a verbal sketch of why exhaustiveness should hold: within Coalg(F), the binary encounter/non-encounter distinction partitions F-coalgebras into Inf vs. (Dist ∪ Expl ∪ Comm); the three-way partition by target (field, geometry, limit) distinguishes the three encounter positions; Ref sits outside Coalg(F) as a distinct structural type (G-coalgebra rather than F-coalgebra) recovered by the "against-F" branch of Paper 1's tree. This file does not re-argue that case and does not claim the theorem is established. The derivation theorem is a named downstream open problem; the schema's contribution is making the five predicates precise enough that the theorem could be attempted. A validator should treat the five positions as proposed categorical distinctions whose exhaustiveness is claimed verbally in Paper 1 and is not re-claimed here.

### What the schema claims

1. Each of the five positions is a structural predicate over works in (C, F, L), not a characterization by example. Positions 1–4 are predicates on F-coalgebras; position 5 is a predicate on (C, F)-situated works that are structured by a modified endofunctor G rather than by F itself.
2. The predicates are categorically specified — they make sense in any (C, F, L) satisfying D1–D3, not only in specific domains.
3. The predicates admit concrete instantiation in at least three domains with categorically distinct carriers (math, music, physics), worked out in detail below.
4. The predicates can be checked against a work in a domain — given a classification of a work into a position, the schema specifies what structural feature of the work supports the classification, beyond resemblance to canonical examples.
5. The schema does *not* claim the five-position derivation is a theorem. That is downstream work.

## Instantiation 1: Pure mathematics — the Pythagorean floor / log₂(3/2)

The carrier with the tightest formal backing. Every structural move in this instantiation is supported either by a classical theorem in number theory or by a claim file with Lean-targeted signatures (see `music-kernel-01-irrationality.md`, `music-kernel-06-baker.md`, `optimal-ntet-continued-fraction.md`).

### Setup

- **C**: the poset category whose objects are finite initial segments of the continued-fraction expansion of α = log₂(3/2), with morphisms the prefix inclusions. Equivalently, objects are finite collections of best rational approximations {p_0/q_0, ..., p_n/q_n} and morphisms extend the collection by one more convergent.
- **F**: one step of continued-fraction expansion — F({p_0/q_0, ..., p_n/q_n}) = {p_0/q_0, ..., p_n/q_n, p_{n+1}/q_{n+1}} where p_{n+1}/q_{n+1} is the next convergent of α. F is an endofunctor on C because prefix inclusion is preserved and each application extends the segment by exactly one convergent.
- **L ⊂ C**: the irrationality locus — the non-existence in C of any finite segment whose closure under F stabilizes, together with the induced Diophantine structure (Baker-type lower bounds on |p - qα| — see `music-kernel-06-baker.md`). L's non-vacuity is the content of the irrationality theorem for α; see `music-kernel-01-irrationality.md` for the signature. In categorical terms, L marks the fact that F has no fixed point in C — no finite segment is closed under F's action.
- **α-coalgebra**: a choice of how to use the continued-fraction expansion as a structural regime — which segment to commit to, how to deploy it, whether to iterate.

### Positions

**Inf (Infrastructure)**: A coalgebra whose carrier X is a single finite segment {p_0/q_0, ..., p_n/q_n} used as-is, without applying F to extend it. The practitioner uses 12/19 (the n = 3 convergent, giving 12-TET's relation to the octave) as the fixed approximation and does not move further down the convergent tree. image(α) ⊂ C \ L — the work's direct reach is in a finite-segment object of C outside L; L lives in the failure-to-close-under-iteration, which this coalgebra's bounded reach never triggers.

**Dist (Distribution)**: A coalgebra whose action maintains two paired convergents — p_n/q_n and p_{n+1}/q_{n+1} simultaneously, alternating above and below α per the best-approximation-of-the-second-kind alternation theorem. The coalgebra engages L (it recognizes the irrationality by holding both sides of the approximation-error at once) and is not entirely in L (it also has representational structure outside the failure-to-close). The two-sided straddling is the Dist-characteristic: error is managed as a bounded oscillation rather than a one-sided approach.

**Expl (Exploitation)**: A coalgebra whose structural move is the optimal N-TET construction — selecting N = q_n (a convergent denominator) as the ring partition whose Diophantine properties are *better* than any smaller N. The convergent denominators 5, 12, 41, 53 are the N-TETs where |N·α − round(N·α)| is strictly less than at any smaller N (see `optimal-ntet-continued-fraction.md`). The practitioner's entire structural claim lies within L's internal geometry — the continued-fraction convergent structure — as the generative principle of the construction. Without L's specific Diophantine texture, the construction is arbitrary; with it, the construction is optimal.

**Comm (Commitment)**: A coalgebra whose carrier is the filtered diagram of all finite segments, with F's action extending each segment to the next, indefinitely. The practitioner's work is the Cauchy sequence of convergents itself, viewed as an approach to the irrational limit. α ∈ ℝ is the colimit apex that would exist in the completion ℝ̂ of C; the colimit does not exist in C (which has finite-segment objects only). Comm is characterized here by the universal property of the filtered diagram — the diagram *is* the practitioner's structural commitment, independent of whether its apex is attained.

**Ref (Refusal)**: The practitioner articulates the work with a different endofunctor G: for example, Baker's linear-forms-in-logarithms framework, where α is treated as a transcendental and the working object is the lower bound |b log 2 + c log 3| > C(b, c) for integers b, c (see `music-kernel-06-baker.md`). G is analytic-number-theoretic rather than continued-fraction-iterative. The structural modification is specific: Baker drops F's prefix-extension structure (the iterative-refinement premise of D2) in favor of a global analytic bound that does not iterate F at all. At the carrier level a Baker-work may still reference rationals in C; at the generative-structure level it is a G-work, not an F-work.

### What this instantiation buys

Three things the pure-math case gives the schema that no other instantiation can:

1. **Actual theorems under every position.** Inf is supported by convergent best-approximation; Dist by the alternation theorem; Expl by the optimal-N-TET claim (Lean signature typechecked, `optimal-ntet-continued-fraction.md`); Comm by the irrationality theorem (Lean signature typechecked, `music-kernel-01-irrationality.md`); Ref by Baker's theorem (cited, `music-kernel-06-baker.md`). No position leans on analogy or empirical calibration alone.
2. **The carrier is as formal as it gets.** C is Diophantine approximations to a specific irrational. Every categorical predicate reduces to a statement about integer or rational arithmetic.
3. **Falsification is sharp.** If any of the five positions fails to admit a concrete coalgebra-instantiation in this carrier, the schema is in trouble. Currently all five are instantiated. If a specialist shows that Comm and Expl collapse in this carrier (e.g., because the convergent sequence itself *is* the optimal-N-TET construction), that is immediate pressure on the schema.

## Instantiation 2: Music — The Fifth

The programme's most developed domain. This instantiation is the bridge between the pure-math case (which shares F with it) and the humanistic domains (which do not).

### Setup

- **C**: the poset category of finite pitch-class sets in ℝ/ℤ with inclusions (Paper 3 § 4 setup; `music-kernel-02-fixed-points.md`).
- **F = D** (the music kernel's functor, named `D` in existing claim files — note this `D` is distinct from the schema's comma `L` and from Paper 3 § 4's D-numbered conditions): the functor D(X) = X ∪ (X + α) for α = log₂(3/2), i.e., transposition-by-fifth closure.
- **L ⊂ C**: the obstruction locus generated by the failure of 12 fifths to close 7 octaves, structurally the Pythagorean comma. `music-kernel-02-fixed-points.md` gives Fix(D) = {∅}; `music-kernel-03-terminal-coalgebra.md` gives no terminal coalgebra for D.
- **α-coalgebra**: a tuning system, temperament, or compositional practice construed as a D-coalgebra on a specific pitch-class carrier.

### Positions

**Inf (Infrastructure)**: diatonic music within a fixed key. The coalgebra's carrier is a 7-note subset of the 12-tone universe; the compositional practice applies D a bounded number of times (within-octave fifth-moves) and does not iterate far enough to reach the twelfth fifth where the Pythagorean comma would appear. Bach's well-tempered works within a single key instantiate this: the work's reach over its extent stays within the 12-tone chromatic set, which lies outside L (L is what appears at the 13th fifth). The comma is present in the enclosing tuning-system closure but not in the compositional reach.

**Dist (Distribution)**: equal temperament. The coalgebra operates on the full 12-tone universe with the Pythagorean comma distributed as 23.46 cents / 12 ≈ 1.955 cents per interval — tension spread across all 12 semitones rather than concentrated at one wolf interval. A practitioner working in ET (Bach's chorales in the *Well-Tempered Clavier* with modulation across keys) instantiates this. image(α) hits L at multiple near-identical locations (one per semitone) and also has structure outside L (the tonal coherence of the modulating compositions themselves).

**Expl (Exploitation)**: Coltrane's *Giant Steps*. The coalgebra uses the major-third cycle of the 12-tone chromatic system — a specific topological feature of L's internal geometry, independently identified by Tymoczko's voice-leading geometry — as its structural generator. Without the comma's specific residue-structure, the major-third cycle's compositional power disappears; the work's coherence depends on the comma's geometry as material. α factors through L ↪ C; the piece's structural move is inside the obstruction.

**Comm (Commitment)**: Pythagorean tuning extended beyond 12 — microtonal / just-intonation extensions that approach log₂(3/2) as an irrational limit by extending D's fifth-chain indefinitely. Harry Partch's 43-tone scale and related microtonal systems approach the limit D's own rules define, within D's own logic, without abandoning the fifth as generator. The coalgebra is an object in a filtered diagram of finite fifth-chains whose colimit apex (if it existed) would be the irrational limit log₂(3/2) ∈ ℝ — which does not exist in C per `music-kernel-03-terminal-coalgebra.md`. Comm is characterized by the universal property of the diagram, not by a completed apex.

**Ref (Refusal)**: Schoenberg's twelve-tone serialism. At the carrier level Schoenberg's rows live in the 12-note chromatic subset of C (which IS a D-coalgebra up to closure), but the operative structure-map is G, not D, where G : C → C is the row-permutation functor (closure under the transpositions, inversions, retrogrades, and retrograde-inversions of a fixed 12-tone row). The structural modification is specific: G drops D's privilege of the fifth as generator (negating the monogenic-by-the-fifth condition that characterizes D); G's existence as an alternative generator of the tonal space is the composition's announced structural argument.

### Carryover from the math instantiation

Because C (math) embeds in C (music) by treating log₂(3/2) as a pitch-class parameter, the math instantiation's Comm position is the formal limit of the music instantiation's Comm position. A practitioner working in microtonal Pythagorean extension is literally instantiating the continued-fraction convergent sequence in a musical carrier. This structural embedding is the source of the programme's strongest cross-domain connection and is one of the reasons the music/math pairing is more than analogy.

## Instantiation 3: Physics — The Wave Function

The domain with a formally proven comma (spectral-gap undecidability, Cubitt, Perez-Garcia & Wolf, *Nature* 2015) and a philosophically contested comma (the measurement problem) simultaneously. Classification of the six canonical interpretations has been pipeline-calibrated; see `db/0114_physics_kernel_validation.sql` for the full classification record.

### Setup

- **C**: the category of quantum systems (Hilbert spaces + unitary maps + measurement structure). The precise object choice is Hilbert space + POVM-augmented state-transition structure; Paper 3 § 6.3 points at the formal setup.
- **F**: the operation coupling unitary evolution with measurement — the Schrödinger equation plus the Born rule. **Caveat specific to this instantiation:** the Born rule is stochastic, which means F as stated is not a clean endofunctor (deterministic morphism assignment) but a Markov kernel or indexed family of morphisms. The physics instantiation's F is therefore the loosest of the three; treating it as an endofunctor requires either (a) enlarging C to a category of probabilistic state-transitions (e.g., the category of quantum channels Chan, where Born-rule measurement becomes a CPTP map), or (b) working with F as a lax / relaxed-structure operation and accepting that strict functoriality is not expected. This file uses (a) as the working assumption; the instantiation's classification of interpretations remains coherent under this enlargement, but the setup is materially less tight than the math and music cases.
- **L ⊂ C**: the measurement problem (the gap between Schrödinger's deterministic evolution and measurement's discontinuous probabilistic outcome), together with the spectral-gap-undecidability locus (Cubitt–Perez-Garcia–Wolf 2015) as the proved formal sub-structure.
- **α-coalgebra**: an interpretation or operational paradigm of quantum mechanics, construed as an F-coalgebra on a particular carrier object (or, in the Refusal case, as a G-structured work).

### Positions

**Inf (Infrastructure)**: Copenhagen interpretation. The coalgebra brackets the measurement problem by postulate — the wave function is an invisible substrate of calculation; collapse is assumed as primitive and never engaged structurally. image(α) ⊂ C \ L at the level of working practice: the Copenhagen physicist's calculations engage unitary evolution (F's deterministic component) on state spaces, and the measurement-problem locus L is held at arm's length by postulate rather than operated on. "Shut up and calculate" is the most complete Inf statement in the physics corpus.

**Dist (Distribution)**: decoherence theory (Zeh, Zurek). The coalgebra distributes the measurement problem across system–environment interactions; L is spread across the environmental entanglement structure rather than concentrated at a single measurement event. image(α) engages L (decoherence engages the measurement problem directly) but is not entirely inside L (the system–environment coupling is structured outside L as well) and distributes across the entanglement-component structure rather than concentrating at one site.

**Expl (Exploitation)**: quantum computing (Feynman, Deutsch, Shor). The coalgebra uses superposition and entanglement — L's specific internal structure — as the computational resource. Without the measurement-problem geometry, quantum advantage does not exist. α factors through L ↪ C; the whole practice lives inside the obstruction.

**Comm (Commitment)**: pilot-wave theory (de Broglie, Bohm). The coalgebra extends Schrödinger's deterministic logic to cover the measurement event itself — measurement becomes emergent from particle guidance rather than a postulated discontinuity. The practitioner extends F's own rules to their structural limit, committing to F's deterministic character even where the standard framework imposes a postulate. The wave function does all structural work; the particles follow.

**Ref (Refusal)**: Many-Worlds interpretation (Everett). The practitioner articulates the work with G = universal unitary evolution without collapse. The structural modification is specific: G drops F's Born-rule / collapse component (which is exactly the non-determinism that made F loose as a functor in the first place). At the carrier level a Many-Worlds practitioner uses the same Hilbert-space state as a Copenhagen practitioner; at the generative-structure level the operative endofunctor is G, not F. G's existence as an alternative generator of the quantum structure is the interpretation's announced structural argument.

### Note on the spectral-gap comma

The formally proven comma in this domain (spectral-gap undecidability) is a Gödel-type result, not a Russell-type result — it is a feature of the measurement / observable structure that generates undecidable questions, not an inconsistency. This matches the programme's general handling of the comma (Paper 3 § 4 last paragraph) and further distinguishes the physics instantiation from, e.g., a hypothetical domain whose "comma" is a Russell-type paradox that forces revision. The five-position schema as stated above is calibrated for Gödel-type commas; its applicability to Russell-type structures is a separate question not taken up in this file.

## Tight spots

Eight places where the schema is currently loose, in roughly descending order of how much a specialist review is likely to tighten them.

**1. Exhaustiveness is asserted verbally in Paper 1 § 3.4, not proved categorically here.** The derivation theorem (that exactly five positions are forced by D1–D3) is named as the downstream open problem; this file states the five predicates but does not prove they are pairwise distinct nor jointly exhaustive. A category theorist may identify that Commitment and Exploitation collapse under a precise formulation, or that a sixth position is required in some carrier.

**2. Comm without a terminal coalgebra.** When F has no terminal coalgebra in Coalg(F) — which is the generic case for the programme's kernels, per `music-kernel-03-terminal-coalgebra.md` — the Commitment predicate is stated as "participation in a filtered diagram whose colimit names the obstructed limit." The formal content of "the colimit names the obstructed limit" when the colimit does not exist in C needs category-theoretic care. Candidate resolutions: (a) pass to the pro-completion of C; (b) work in the enriched setting where the colimit exists by construction; (c) redefine Commitment by the universal property of the diagram rather than by convergence to an apex (this file has taken step (c) tentatively in the schema wording; validator pressure likely determines whether (a) or (b) should replace it).

**3. Refusal's G-modification constraint.** The Refusal predicate says "G is obtained from F by a specific structural modification — typically the dropping or negation of one of D1–D3." *Typically* is honest but load-bearing — a validator will push for a closed specification of the allowed G-modifications, without which Ref risks sliding toward "any non-F coalgebra on C." Candidate tightening: Refusal requires that G be obtained from F by exactly one of the following operations — (i) drop one of D1–D3 (most commonly D3, the fixed-point / self-limiting structure); (ii) replace F's action on some component by a structurally distinct action that does not generate L; (iii) restrict F to the complement of its fixed-point subcategory. The music-kernel Schoenberg case and the physics-kernel Many-Worlds case both instantiate (i). The schema currently names the requirement and gives examples but does not prove the list is exhaustive.

**4. Distribution's "multiple components" predicate.** The Dist predicate requires L to admit a meaningful component structure so that "hits multiple components of L" is non-vacuous. For the math instantiation (L = irrationality locus with Diophantine Farey-level structure) and for the music instantiation (L = Pythagorean comma with 12-fifth + 7-octave residue structure), components are straightforward. For the physics instantiation (L = measurement problem, with spectral-gap locus as a proved sub-structure), what counts as a "component" is less sharp. A validator may argue that the Dist / Inf distinction degenerates when L lacks a natural decomposition.

**5. Work-level vs. corpus-level scope.** Paper 1 § 3.4's "ontological note on Commitment" flags that Commitment may be irreducibly corpus-level — recoverable only from the practitioner's body of work, not from a single piece. This file's predicates are stated over a single coalgebra α : X → F(X), which presumes work-level recovery. If Commitment requires corpus-level data, the schema as stated is under-specified in this axis and would need to be re-stated over a category of corpora rather than works. Paper 1 § 3.4's two open responses (kernel refinement vs. topology extension) remain relevant.

**6. The specific mapping to D1–D3 versus to the Paper 1 decision-tree.** Paper 3 § 4 states D1–D4 as non-triviality / non-idempotence / fixed-point / comma-generation; Paper 1 § 3.4 derives the five positions from a decision tree over target-space partitions. The schema in this file reads the decision tree against the D1–D3 machinery, treating the decision-tree partition as categorically induced by the comma / fixed-point structure. A validator may conclude that the two framings are not quite reading the same structure and that one of them needs to be preferred or re-stated to match the other. A likely outcome: Paper 3 v10.0 keeps the D1–D4 spine and incorporates the schema as a refinement of D4, while Paper 1 § 3.4 reframes its decision-tree argument as the structural intuition for why D4 takes the five-position shape.

**7. Physics F is not strictly an endofunctor.** Named above in the physics setup; reiterated here because it is the most technically specific loose spot. The Born rule introduces stochasticity that strict functoriality does not support. The schema's use of (a) "enlarge to Chan, the category of quantum channels" is a plausible but unverified resolution. A mathematical physicist is the right validator here, not a pure category theorist.

**8. AI-synthesis origin.** This schema is an AI-assisted synthesis of Paper 1 § 3.4, Paper 3 § 4, Paper 1 v11.5's Lawvere addition, the music-kernel claim cluster, and external readings of Lawvere / Lambek / Yanofsky. No category theorist has been consulted prior to filing this claim. Consistent with `docs/observations/validation-architecture-outcomes.md` AI-synthesis-origin disclosure discipline.

## Falsification conditions

Six ways the schema could be shown to fail, in descending order of severity.

**1. A non-trivial work in any domain occupies a position not reducible to one of the five.** Paper 1 § 3.3's existing falsifiability condition. The schema inherits it; if classification consistently fails for a class of works in a well-specified kernel, the schema is wrong or the kernel definition is wrong (Paper 1's default reading is kernel misspecification; the schema does not change that default).

**2. Two of the five positions collapse under any precise categorical formulation.** The strongest specific candidates are Commitment / Exploitation (do they collapse when the comma *is* the limit — the case where L carries its own colimit as internal structure?) and Distribution / Exploitation (do they collapse when L's "internal geometry" is the same structure as its "components"?). A particular pressure point in the math instantiation: Expl (using q_n as optimal N-TET) and Comm (using the filtered diagram of all q_n) both lean on the convergent sequence; the schema distinguishes them by whether a finite q_n is selected as a finished structure (Expl) or the whole diagram is committed to as a limiting process (Comm), and a reviewer may argue this distinction is pragmatic rather than categorical. If the pair collapses, the schema reduces to four positions and the derivation needs to be re-argued.

**3. Comm requires ν but domains exist where F has no terminal coalgebra and works clearly occupy Comm.** The music kernel is such a domain (`music-kernel-03-terminal-coalgebra.md`). If Commitment is well-defined there only by analogy rather than by the filtered-diagram universal-property predicate, Comm's categorical content is weaker than claimed.

**4. Refusal's G is under-constrained.** If the schema cannot narrow the allowed G-modifications beyond "some structural change to F," Refusal becomes a label for a residual rather than a structural position. The Refusal / Exploitation contrast in particular — a composer who refuses tonality altogether (Ref) vs. a composer who uses the tonal system's comma against itself (Expl) — depends on G being structurally specified. Tight spot 3's (i)/(ii)/(iii) candidate list addresses this but is not proved exhaustive.

**5. The cross-domain uniformity fails.** The schema bites in one domain (e.g., math) but breaks in another (e.g., physics) because the kernel's structural features differ in ways the schema does not abstract over. A validator may identify that the physics kernel's coupling of unitary evolution with measurement produces a coalgebra structure too different from the math or music kernels for the same predicates to apply cleanly. If cross-domain uniformity fails, the programme's central claim reverts from structural prediction to domain-by-domain convergence.

**6. The schema overclaims what Lambek / Lawvere can support.** The Commitment predicate implicitly uses Lambek's lemma (terminal coalgebra structure map is iso) and the programme's Lawvere reformulation (`lawvere-unification-of-formal-groundings.md`). If the category-theoretic machinery invoked is stronger than what the programme's kernels support, the schema is over-applied even where its content is coherent.

For each of the five positions and for each of the three instantiations, a validator should state:

1. **Predicate is coherent as stated** — the structural condition is categorically meaningful over (C, F, L) satisfying D1–D3.
2. **Predicate is coherent with amendment** — the condition is close but needs specific refinement (propose it).
3. **Predicate collapses into another** — the condition as stated is structurally indistinguishable from a neighbor position under precise formulation; propose either the collapse or the tightening that avoids it.

5. **Instantiation is coherent / incoherent** — for each of the three instantiations, is the claimed carrier (C, F, L) well-formed, and do the five positions instantiate as claimed?

A validator who confirms the schema's three instantiations as coherent while flagging one or more tight spots for refinement is the most likely and most useful response.

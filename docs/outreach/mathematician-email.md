# Outreach draft — Email to a mathematician (category theory / number theory)

**Purpose.** Request targeted, bounded-time verification of the six-point music-kernel formalization from a mathematician not known to the author.
**Target recipient.** Academic mathematicians in category theory, number theory, or logic; specifically people who might respond to a cold email on the basis of the specificity and brevity of the request.
**Length target.** ≤ 400 words.
**Tone.** Direct, specific, non-imposing.
**Related:** [`../../validation/claims/music-kernel-umbrella.md`](../../validation/claims/music-kernel-umbrella.md).

---

## Subject

> Bounded request: 1-hour verification of six elementary claims (category theory + number theory)

## Body

Dear Prof. [Surname],

I am an independent researcher writing to ask whether you would consider spending about one hour verifying a specific set of six elementary mathematical claims, on whatever terms you find appropriate. I am not asking for endorsement of the broader project the claims support; only for verification that the claims themselves are correctly stated and supported.

The claims are listed at [https://github.com/thefalsework/papers/blob/main/validation/claims/music-kernel-umbrella.md](https://github.com/thefalsework/papers/blob/main/validation/claims/music-kernel-umbrella.md). In brief:

1. Irrationality of `α = log₂(3/2)` via the fundamental theorem of arithmetic.
2. `Fix(D) = {∅}` for the endofunctor `D(X) = X ∪ (X + α)` on finite subsets of `ℝ / ℤ`, by a cardinality argument.
3. No terminal coalgebra for `D`, by Lambek's lemma.
4. The colimit of `D`-iteration escapes the ambient category of finite subsets, by Weyl equidistribution (1916).
5. A `ℤ / 12ℤ` quotient structure with a suspected error in an existing draft that I would like your opinion on.
6. Baker's 1966 theorem applied to `|12 log 3 − 19 log 2|` as an effective Diophantine lower bound on the Pythagorean comma.

Each claim has a self-contained one-page statement at the URL above. Your options for a response are:

- **Confirm as stated.** One line per point.
- **Confirm with amendment.** Propose a precise rewording.
- **Flag an error.** Identify what is wrong.
- **Decline a point.** "Out of scope" is an acceptable answer.

A complete response to all six is ideal; a response to any subset is useful. Partial responses will be credited by name with your permission.

This work is part of an open research programme on structural incompleteness across domains ([https://github.com/thefalsework/papers](https://github.com/thefalsework/papers), CC-BY-4.0). I cannot offer monetary compensation but am happy to write a reference letter for graduate-student contributors, to credit you publicly in the revision note that incorporates your feedback, and to discuss the broader framework on request.

If this is of no interest, no response is needed and I will not follow up. If you know a colleague for whom this would be more appropriate, I would welcome a referral.

Thank you for considering.
